The hidden cost of impacts

Most concussions go undetected, misdiagnosed, or ignored.

Kids don't always tell you when they're hurt, and a sideline call is a judgment made after the fact. The real danger isn't the first hit — it's the second one, before a young brain has recovered. Repeated undetected impacts are linked to memory loss, depression, and CTE. And it starts long before the pros.

The moat · per-athlete risk

Not a threshold. A probability.

A single g-force cutoff is wrong for almost everyone. Axon scores each impact as a probability of concussion, then bends that curve to the individual athlete — the part that's hard to copy.

COMBINED PROBABILITY OF CONCUSSION · ROWSON & DUMA, 2013
R = 1 / (1 + e−(β0 + β1a + β2α + β3aα))
a = peak linear acceleration (g)  ·  α = peak rotational acceleration (rad/s²)  ·  the interaction term aα is what single-axis sensors miss
Neck strength
